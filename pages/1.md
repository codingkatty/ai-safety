# **AI companions**
**How teens use chatbots for schoolwork**
- About four-in-ten or more report having used them to get help <mark>researching a topic or solving math problems. </mark>
By comparison, teens are less likely to turn to AI for help ==editing something they wrote==. Still, 35% of teens say they have done this.


## Research
	![alt text](https://media.istockphoto.com/vectors/isometric-map-of-united-kingdom-detailed-vector-illustration-vector-id540501772?k=20&m=540501772&s=612x612&w=0&h=313DJi1POmYW2Qa3ddFpdllWzebiRtlWPlQv5ceoyPE=.jpg)
- According to recent reports, four in five (79%) UK online adolescents (aged 13–17; Ofcom, 2023), 66% of Belgian adolescents (aged 10–18; Apenstaartjaren, 2024), and 51% of US-based youth (aged 14–22; Hopelab, 2024) **had used at least once a generative AI tool** (e.g., ChatGPT, Snapchat’s My AI, Midjourney, and DALL-E).
- According to a report from the nonprofit Common Sense, *72 percent* of teens have used **AI companions, or chatbots** 
designed to have personal or emotionally supportive conversations, and more than half of teens use them regularly.
