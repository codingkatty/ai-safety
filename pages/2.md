<br><p style="font-size:30px;"><strong><mark>Cyberbullying with Deepfakes</mark></strong></p>
<p> : The New York Times reported on an epidemic of deepfake nudes being created and shared in middle and high schools. Teen boys use AI software to <b>create explicit images of female classmates, using their real, identifiable faces</b>. </p>
<p style="font-size:30px;"><strong><mark>Diminished Creativity</mark></strong></p>
<p>: Educators feel that AI could <b>impair young people’s creativity and self-confidence</b>.</p>
<p style="font-size:30px;"><strong><mark>Lack of Meaningful Human Connection</mark></strong></p>
<p> : These digital interactions presents a significant risk of “living in an echo chamber”—hearing only what you want to hear. That’s because AI is programmed to <b>reinforce a person’s beliefs and opinions</b>.</p>
